Theme

How does your power give you comfort, and how does your comfort give you power? This question is the main theme of Sawubona, and we invite you to reflect on how these aspects of your life interact with each other throughout your creative process. To stir your inspiration and to enrich our theme, we ask you to consider the following questions: 

  • What does power mean to you? 
  • What does comfort mean to you? 
  • Where do you get power from? Does your power bring you comfort?
  • Where do you find comfort? Does your comfort empower you?
  • How have you used your power? How do you want to use it?
  • Where do you see power around you? What form does it take? 
  • How do you use power to bring comfort to yourself or others, or how would you?

Eligibility

Sawubona has a focused purpose of uplifting members of our community that may be overlooked otherwise. Therefore, to be eligible for submission, you must be 16 to 25 years of age and a resident of the Triangle area. The Triangle area is Durham, Chapel Hill, and Raleigh, as well as any towns or suburbs immediately between the three cities. 

To achieve our purpose and guide the exploration of Sawubona's theme, we will be prioritizing BIPOC and LGBT artists throughout our selection process. Anyone meeting the age and location criteria may submit pieces for considerations, but please understand that our zine will be created with these identities at the forefront of the conversation.

Dates and Details

We will be accepting submissions until January 11th, 2021. 
We hope to notify selected artists by January 27th, 2021.

Artists may submit pieces of 2D or 3D art, poetry or short written works, or recordings of performance art. Artists may submit up to 3 pieces of work for consideration. For multi-piece submissions, each piece may be of any accepted medium and can be a standalone piece or part of a collection. We ask that you provide one artist's statement for each standalone piece or collection.
